Q1. Which part of the nervous system includes the brain and spinal cord?





Explanation: The CNS includes the brain and spinal cord, responsible for processing and integrating information.

Q2. Afferent neurons carry signals:





Explanation: Afferent neurons carry sensory signals from the body to the CNS.

Q3. Which of the following is part of the peripheral nervous system?





Explanation: Cranial nerves are part of the PNS, while the others are central structures.

Q4. The somatic nervous system primarily controls:





Explanation: The somatic nervous system controls voluntary movements via skeletal muscle.

Q5. Which structure acts as a relay station between sensory input and the cerebral cortex?





Explanation: The thalamus relays sensory signals to the appropriate cortical areas.

Q6. Which division of the nervous system is responsible for involuntary control of internal organs?





Explanation: The autonomic nervous system regulates involuntary functions like heart rate and digestion.

Q7. Which cells form the myelin sheath in the central nervous system?





Explanation: Oligodendrocytes myelinate axons in the CNS, while Schwann cells do so in the PNS.

Q8. Which of the following best describes the function of interneurons?





Explanation: Interneurons serve as connectors between sensory and motor pathways within the CNS.

Q9. Which part of the brain is primarily responsible for coordination and balance?





Explanation: The cerebellum integrates sensory input to fine-tune motor activity and maintain balance.

Q10. Damage to the spinal cord would most directly affect which function?





Explanation: The spinal cord transmits motor signals from the brain to the body; damage impairs movement.
